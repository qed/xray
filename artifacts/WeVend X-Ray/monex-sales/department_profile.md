## 1. Department Identity

**Department Name:** MonexGroup Sales
**Department Lead:** John Farrugia, VP of Business Development

**Mission:**
To build a sales team that operates with integrity and teamwork in order to provide the most innovative payment products in the industry to our partners.

**What the Department Does:**
MonexGroup Sales is responsible for acquiring and growing relationships with payment solution partners across two primary segments: unattended payments (car wash, vending, parking, kiosks) and attended payments (traditional POS, eCommerce). The team sources leads, manages the full sales cycle from outreach to close, handles onboarding paperwork, and maintains ongoing account relationships post-sale.

## 6. Sales Workflow (End-to-End)

### Step 1 — Lead Generation & Capture
- Inbound leads (website, Google, Facebook ads) auto-generate a Telelead record via web form submission
- Outbound leads (email outreach, mailers, trade shows, influencers, forums) are entered manually
- Marketing supplies additional inbound leads that drop directly into Teleleads

### Step 2 — Lead Assignment
- **Manual process**: John Farrugia, Henry, or Brendan review new Teleleads and manually assign to the appropriate rep based on vertical
- No auto-assign rules exist in Teleleads
- **Key risk**: Off-hours coverage gap. John personally handles lead assignment on evenings and weekends. If unavailable, leads can sit unassigned

### Step 3 — Rep Outreach
- Rep is expected to contact newly assigned leads **within 1 hour** of assignment
- Each rep uses their own templates, adapted into their own language — no standardized script
- Follow-up cadence standard: **48 hours** between touches

### Step 4 — Quoting
- Rep prepares a quote using a standardized quote document
- **Time to quote:** ~10-15 minutes
- **Pricing authority:** Reps can quote independently within pricing thresholds. If discounting below threshold or offering free devices/accessories to close a deal, **approval from John Farrugia is required**
- Manager approvals are infrequent; John is accessible via phone/text/email

### Step 5 — Close & Paperwork
- Customer completes a questionnaire
- Rep manually builds the application based on the questionnaire responses — **~1 hour per deal** (automation in progress to eliminate this step)
- Application is loaded into **DocuSign** for the customer to sign; documents include:
  - MonexGroup Terms
  - Acquirer Terms
  - Lease documents (occasionally)
- Customer must also submit:
  - Proof of business
  - Two forms of ID
  - Bank letter or void cheque
- **Common blockers:** Customers often don't know their exact legal business name or have banking information that doesn't exactly match — leading to **2-3 additional follow-up touchpoints** per deal to collect clean documentation

### Step 6 — Admin Processing
- Completed paperwork is submitted to the Admin team (part of Operations)
- Admin manually re-keys deal information into **three separate systems:**
  1. **Phorge** (MonexGroup internal system)
  2. **Acquirer system**
  3. **Hardware partner system** (unattended deals only)
- **Time per deal:** 60-90 minutes
- No system integration exists — all data entry is manual, creating significant error risk

### Step 7 — Device Programming & Shipping

| Market | Programming | Fulfillment | Typical Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Canada** | Done in-house at MonexGroup main office | Shipped directly from MonexGroup office | 1-2 weeks from admin completion |
| **US** | Parameters sent to hardware partner **Castles** who programs remotely | Castles ships directly to customer | 2-3 weeks from admin completion |

**Canada Handoff:** Admin → Service Team → Ship
**US Handoff:** Admin → Castles parameters → Castles programs and ships

### Step 8 — Go Live & Post-Sale Account Management
- Reps remain involved with accounts after go-live — no hard handoff to a separate account management team
- **Attended accounts:** Minimal ongoing touchpoints after go-live
- **Unattended accounts:** More frequent involvement, often due to integration challenges with legacy/older devices in the field
- Internal technical support exists but sometimes requires rep involvement to bridge communication with the customer ("hand holding")
- **Time cost:** Reps spend approximately **3-5 hours per week** on post-sale technical support calls and escalations

### External Dependencies

| Partner | Dependency | Friction Level |
|---|---|---|
| **Castles (US hardware partner)** | Programs and ships devices for all US deals | **High** — slow deployment (up to 3 weeks), archaic inventory management, programming errors occur |

### Key Friction Points in Handoffs
- **Castles deployment**: Slow turnaround, outdated inventory management, programming errors that MONEX service must fix remotely — strains customer relationships
- **Finance → Sales notification**: Payment confirmation is not consistently pushed to reps; manual follow-up required
- **Admin re-keying**: Three separate systems with no integration creates manual burden and error risk
